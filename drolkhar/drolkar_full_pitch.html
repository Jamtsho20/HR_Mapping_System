The problem
The invisible gap in every working mother's day
School ends at 1pm. Work ends at 5pm.
Four hours with no verified, safe, culturally appropriate place for the child. This gap is forcing mothers to leave jobs, miss promotions, or leave children unsupervised.
The journey to care is untracked
No one is responsible for walking the child from school to a care center. Mothers have no way to know if their child arrived safely — especially from work.
Poor mothers are excluded by fees
Informal workers and daily wage earners cannot afford even small care fees — yet they are the mothers who need care the most.
Cultural identity eroding in urban areas
Children in Thimphu are growing up with less Dzongkha, fewer traditional stories, and weaker connection to Bhutanese values — because no structured time exists for cultural transmission after school.
Government has no real-time data
Dzongkhag offices rely on annual surveys. No live picture of child welfare, caregiver quality, or family well-being exists anywhere in Bhutan today.
The solution
Drolkar — four layers, one platform
Layer 1 · The Care Network
Verified local women — retired teachers, grandmothers, trained youth — register as caregivers or micro-center hosts on Drolkar. Mothers find, book, and pay them through the app or SMS.
Layer 2 · The Draktshen Escort System
A trained, background-checked community walker collects children from the school Collection Corner and walks them to the care center as a group. The mother is notified at every step via SMS.
Layer 3 · The Physical Care Card
Every child — including those without a smartphone — gets a laminated QR card. The caregiver scans it at arrival, triggering an instant SMS to the mother: "Pema arrived safely. 2:18pm."
Layer 4 · The GNH Dashboard
The dzongkhag sees live data — well-being scores, subsidy routing, caregiver quality, missing-child alerts — replacing annual paper surveys with real-time national intelligence.

Child safety system
Complete chain of custody — school to center
1
School Collection Corner — 1:00pm
School staff supervise enrolled children for 15 min after school. Drolkar MOU with school — one page, no cost. Children wait as a named group.
School responsible
2
Draktshen escort departs — 1:15pm
Verified community walker collects the group. Taps "Walk Started" → mother gets SMS: "Pema's group left school. 8 children. Karma Wangmo escorting." Escort carries phone, not child.
Draktshen responsible
3
QR card scanned at center — 1:30pm
Caregiver scans each child's card. Mother gets SMS: "Pema arrived safely. 1:32pm. Changzamtog." Responsibility transfers to caregiver — timestamped, logged, irrefutable.
Caregiver responsible
4
Auto-alert if card not scanned — T+20min
If expected scan doesn't happen, platform alerts mother, Draktshen, and dzongkhag child welfare officer simultaneously. No one has to notice — the system catches it.
3-way auto-alert
5
Pickup confirmed — 5:00pm
Mother scans her own QR at pickup. Child's record closes for the day. Full log available to dzongkhag if ever needed.
Full daily record
Rural "Nangkha" micro-centers
In villages too remote for a central center, a trusted local woman hosts 3–5 children at home — registered as a micro-center. Same QR system, same SMS alerts, same GNH tracking. No building required.
Technology stack
Cutting-edge tools, grounded in Bhutan's reality
AI-powered caregiver matching
Instead of a simple search, Drolkar uses a lightweight ML model to match mothers with caregivers based on location, language, child's age, schedule, and past ratings — similar to how Spotify matches music to mood.
New · differentiator
QR + NFC dual-mode check-in
Every care card has both a QR code (works on any camera phone) and an NFC chip (tap-to-check-in for modern phones). Future-proofed for when Bhutan's smartphone penetration grows. NFC cards cost Nu. 15 each.
New · inclusive
SMS-first architecture (no internet required)
The entire alert system — child departure, arrival, missing-child — runs over basic SMS using a Bhutan Telecom gateway. Works in every dzongkhag with mobile signal, even without data. Built on the Twilio model adapted for BT network.
Inclusion · rural-ready
GNH scoring algorithm
A composite well-being score built from: parent satisfaction, child mood check-ins, cultural session completion, caregiver attendance, and subsidy uptake — weighted by the four GNH pillars. First time GNH is computed in real time from behavioral data.
Unique to Bhutan · world first
Offline-first mobile app (PWA)
Built as a Progressive Web App — works without internet, syncs when connection returns. Caregivers in remote areas can log attendance offline; it uploads when they reach signal. No app store needed — runs in any browser.
No app store · lightweight
Dzongkha voice interface
For caregivers and mothers who are not comfortable reading, Drolkar integrates a basic Dzongkha voice assistant — speak to book, speak to confirm arrival. Built on open-source speech models fine-tuned for Dzongkha phonemes.
